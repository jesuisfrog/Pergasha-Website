Cryofusion

Cryofusion is the ability to use water motes to form ice. This affinity alters the bearer with a penetrating glare, sharper voice, or chilly disposition.
Cryofusists exude a cooling demeanor and focus, allowing them stay level-headed even in the most uncomfortable conditions. They have advantage on any saves or checks against environmental effects and can keep perishables that they carry preserved for twice as long. Additionally, by taking this infusion, a cryofusist gains the Iceweaving ability.

  • Iceweaving ?

    • Requirements: None

    • Activation: 1 action to 10 minutes
    • Duration: Concentration, up to 1 hour
    • You are able to chill or freeze water and similar liquids. You must have a source of liquid within 10 feet of you to activate this ability. The amount of liquid you can affect and the time required to do so is based on your Waterweaving ability: Tier I requires 1 action to freeze, Tier II requires 1 minute, and Tier III requires 10 minutes. Alternatively, you can reduce the time requirement to 1 action by expending 1 Hit Die for each tier above I. Once you have initiated this ability, you must stay within 30 feet of the ice and hold concentration to continue controlling it. You can manipulate the ice in the following ways: Shape and Move: As an action, you can create simple shapes (or break them apart) and animate the ice. For example, you can create a solid sphere of ice that you shift and move up to 30 feet per turn. You are able to concentrate on this manipulation for up to 1 hour. Once this ability ends, the sphere reverts back to its natural state, falling to the ground or being held in a prepared vessel. Toss: As a bonus action, you can toss ice you are controlling at a creature or area. The tier of ice you toss determines the area you can target, any extra effects, and the amount of bludgeoning damage dealt (refer to the end of this description). You still control the ice regardless of the results of the attack and can continue using your bonus action to toss it. Any creatures in the area of your toss attack must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a success, creatures take half the damage but suffer no other effects. The DC for this saving throw is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier. Waterweaving Tier I: Target must be an area or creature you can see within 30 feet of you. You cannot toss ice through solid objects. Any creature in a 5-foot radius of this attack takes 1d10 bludgeoning damage and are pushed back 5 feet on a failed Dexterity saving throw. Waterweaving Tier II: Target must be an area or creature you can see within 60 feet of you. You cannot toss ice through solid objects. Any creature in a 15-foot radius of this attack takes 3d10 bludgeoning damage and are knocked prone on a failed Dexterity saving throw. Waterweaving Tier III: Target must be an area or creature you can see within 60 feet of you. You cannot toss ice through solid objects. Any creature in a 15-foot radius of this attack takes 5d10 bludgeoning damage and are knocked prone on a failed Dexterity saving throw. In addition, you can choose to leave shards of ice on the ground, creating difficult terrain that deals 1d4 piercing damage to any creature walking through a space with the ice shards. Doing so ends your control over the ice.
